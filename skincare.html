The Secret to Getting a Dewy Look

Sparkling right from the start
Kaitlyn Smith
22 February 2022

THE AUTUMN ISSUE

CLICK TO READ
STEP 1: CLEANSE

While you're dreaming up your next tropical getaway, you can treat your complexion to a mango-scented escape every night right at home. As the name suggests, Glow Recipe's Papaya Enzyme Cleansing Balm is spiked with papaya enzymes to gently slough dead skin cells and refine the appearance of pores, while papaya seed oil adds a dose of hydration. The K-beauty-inspired brand also infuses antioxidant-rich blueberries, similar to the brand's water-based cleanser, to shield skin against the damaging effects of pollution and UV radiation.

STEP 2: EXFOLIATE

Allure editors have already confirmed that Sunday Riley's Good Genes Lactic Acid Treatment lives up to the hype, and board-certified dermatologist Debra Jaliman agrees. "The lactic acid plumps the skin," she tells Allure. According to Jaliman, the glowy skin-inducing treatment is infused with licorice extract to diminish the appearance of dark spots and hyperpigmentation. There's also aloe to eliminate redness, and lemongrass, which gets rid of bacteria with its natural antiseptic properties.

STEP 3: TONER

Also per Dr. Hartman, the Fenty Skin Fat Water Pore-Refining Toner Serum is a great choice for all skin types since it "deliver[s] a brightening and toning effect without stripping away too much oil." The product — which is one-part toner, one-part serum — also contains niacinamide, which the dermatologist credits for its skin-smoothing and pore-minimizing properties.

STEP 4: SERUM

La Roche-Posay's Hyalu B5 Pure Hyaluronic Acid Face Serum contains, not one, but two types of pure hyaluronic acid — and vitamin B5 — which work together to intensely hydrate skin immediately and over time. Safe for all skin types, including sensitive complexions, it's an excellent drugstore option to try if you're in the market for a new hydrating treatment.

STEP 5: MASKS

A whopping 25-percent alpha hydroxy acid formula packs a serious punch in Drunk Elephant's T.L.C. Sukari Babyfacial, which quickly won over skin-care fanatics with its gentle yet effective approach to at-home exfoliation. "Ingredients such as matcha and milk thistle contain potent antioxidant and anti-pollution properties, which help to condition and soothe redness," Shereene Idriss, a board-certified dermatologist in New York City, tells Allure.

STEP 6: SUNSCREEN

The Supergoop Glowscreen SPF 40 is technically a makeup primer that also provides SPF 40 protection. It contains antioxidants like lavender to shield the skin from free radicals and cocoa peptides to protect the skin from blue light. Its iridescent finish will bring you one step closer to getting a natural-looking, dewy glow.
